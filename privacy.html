Privacy Policy & Terms

Last updated: July 2026 · Gritto Beta

Gritto is a personal project built to help young athletes practice smarter — pick a sport, get AI-generated drills, track a daily routine, and see your progress over time. It's currently a small beta, shared with a limited group of invited testers, not a public commercial product.

What we collect

  • If you sign in with Google: your name, email address, and profile photo
  • Your activity: sports, drills, routines, streaks, and how long you practiced
  • If you upload a video: one still image (thumbnail) pulled from your clip, plus the AI's written feedback about it — we do not keep the video file itself
  • Feedback or bug reports you send us through the app
  • Basic technical info (device type, browser, app version) if you use the built-in debug tool
  • If you turn on reminders: a way to send your device a push notification

How we use it

  • To run the app's features — tracking streaks, showing your history, generating drills, sending reminders
  • To fix bugs and improve Gritto

We do not sell your information or share it with advertisers.

Where it's stored

Your data is stored using Supabase (a database service) and processed using Anthropic's Claude AI (to generate drills and analyze videos). These are third-party services with their own security and privacy practices, separate from Gritto.

Your choices

  • Clear your video history or activity log anytime in Settings
  • Turn reminders off anytime in Settings
  • Ask to have your account and data deleted by using the feedback form in Settings

Kids and privacy

Gritto is built with young athletes in mind. If you're a parent with questions about your child's data, or would like it removed, please reach out using the feedback form in Settings.

Not a substitute for coaching

Gritto provides general coaching information generated by AI. It is not a substitute for guidance from a qualified, in-person coach, trainer, or medical professional.

Changes to this page

As Gritto grows, this policy may change. We'll update this page if it does.

This is a straightforward policy written for a personal beta project, not formal legal advice. If Gritto ever grows into a public or commercial product, it should get a proper legal review — especially given specific rules that can apply to apps used by kids.

Questions?

Use the "Report a bug or send feedback" box in Settings.
